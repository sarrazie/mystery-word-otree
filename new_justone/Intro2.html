Each round is divided into six phases:

1. All players except the guesser are informed of the secret word they need to provide a clue pair for.

2. Without communicating, each clue giver generates clue pairs individually.

3. Next, the clue givers come together and comment on their previously written clue pairs.

4. Afterwards, each clue giver has time to revise their clue pairs.

5. Now, the clue givers collectively decide which single clue pair they want to pass to the guesser.

6. Before the guesser sees them, identical clue pairs are removed. The guesser has only one attempt to guess the mystery word.

After the round, the group is informed whether the guesser correctly guessed the mystery word. The next round begins, and another group member becomes the guesser.
