Identical Clue Pairs:

  • Your group's clue pair contains at least one word that is identical to a word in a clue pair from another group.
    • Example: If one group writes Mouse and another group also writes Mouse, then both groups' complete clue pairs are considered identical.
  • Your group's clue pair contains at least one word that shares the same root word as a word in a clue pair from another group.
    • Example: If one group writes Player and another group writes Playfield, then both groups' complete clue pairs are considered identical.
  • The clue pair contain words that are variations of the same word: Plural forms, gender differentiations, and spelling errors.
    • Example: If one group writes Bakery and another group writes Bakeries, then both groups' complete clue pairs are considered identical.
